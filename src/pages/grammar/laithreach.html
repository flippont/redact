One syllable verbs

The present tense in Irish, you check the amount of syllables and broad and slender just like in the past tense. For the one syllable verbs you then use aim(for broad verbs)/im(for slender verbs) for first person, and ann(for broad verbs)/eann(for slender verbs) as the endings of second and third person verbs.
For the We/Us part, you use aimid/imid as the endings.
For a question, you use "An" and a urú
For the saorbhriathar (The Free Verb), use tar/tear as the endings.

Two syllable verbs

For the two syllable verbs, you use aím/ím and aíonn/íonn for the endings respective of their pronouns.
The big difference between the one and two syllable verbs are the endings. You have to remove the endings first before adding a new one.
Here are the endings to remove:
- For verbs that finish in aigh/igh, remove the aigh/igh
- For verbs that don't finish in aigh/igh, remove the last vowel/vowels (E.g. Oscail => Oscl, Imir => Imr)
